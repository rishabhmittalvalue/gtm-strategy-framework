# Module 1: Market Sizing Framework

## Overview
A rigorous market sizing approach using both top-down (industry reports) and bottom-up (unit economics) methodologies to triangulate the real opportunity.

---

## Top-Down Analysis

### Step 1: Define the Total Addressable Market (TAM)
- Start with the broadest market category (e.g., "AI-powered enterprise software")
- Source: Industry research reports (Gartner, IDC, McKinsey)
- Sanity check: Does this number pass the "so what" test for investors?

**Example:**
- Global AI enterprise software market: $47B (2025), growing at 28% CAGR
- Source: Gartner 2024 AI Market Forecast

### Step 2: Narrow to Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)
- Filter by: geography, company size, industry vertical, use case fit
- Apply "can we realistically sell to this segment" filter

**Example:**
- Mid-market US/Canada companies (500-5000 employees) in tech/SaaS: $6.2B
- Filtering criteria: English-language, cloud-first, ops budget > $500K

### Step 3: Define Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)
- Based on realistic GTM capacity in Year 1-2
- Inputs: sales headcount, ACV, sales cycle length, conversion rates

**Example:**
- With 3 AEs, 90-day sales cycle, $50K ACV, 20% win rate: ~$8.5M ARR Year 2
- SOM framing: $85M in 5 years = 1.4% of SAM (conservative and credible)

## Bottom-Up Analysis

### Unit Economics Approach
```
Total Companies in ICP = 12,500
Reachable via outbound (Year 1) = 2,500 (20%)
Qualified pipeline (20% conversion) = 500
Closing rate = 15%
Deals closed Year 1 = 75
Average Contract Value = $48,000
Year 1 ARR = $3.6M
```

### Cohort Growth Assumption
| Year | New Customers | Churned | Net Customers | ARR |
|------|--------------|---------|---------------|-----|
| 1    | 75           | 5       | 70            | $3.6M |
| 2    | 150          | 12      | 208           | $10.8M |
| 3    | 280          | 22      | 466           | $24.2M |

---

## Key Questions to Stress-Test Your Sizing

1. Are you sizing the market you can win, or the market that exists?
2. What % of TAM is actually reachable with your current GTM motion?
3. What's the implied market share needed to hit your 5-year revenue target? Is it credible?
4. What would have to be true for your SOM to be 2x larger?

---

## Common Mistakes
- Using TAM as a proxy for opportunity (rookie mistake)
- Ignoring market timing (is the buyer ready to buy NOW?)
- Forgetting to account for sales capacity constraints
- Not stress-testing with bottom-up numbers
