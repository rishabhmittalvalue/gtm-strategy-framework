# Go-To-Market Strategy Framework for AI-Native B2B Products

> A strategic framework and GTM playbook built for launching AI-native B2B products into competitive markets. Developed as part of MBA coursework and independent research at Kellogg School of Management.

---

## The Problem

Most AI startups build impressive technology but struggle to answer three critical GTM questions:
1. **Who is the right buyer, and why do they care right now?**
2. **What market are we actually competing in, and how big is the real opportunity?**
3. **How do we win against entrenched incumbents with limited resources?**

This framework is a structured approach to answering all three.

---

## Framework Overview

This project contains a 5-part GTM strategy framework applied to an AI-native B2B use case:

| Module | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **1. Market Sizing** | TAM / SAM / SOM analysis with bottom-up and top-down validation |
| **2. Buyer Persona & ICP** | Ideal Customer Profile definition, pain point mapping, and decision-maker analysis |
| **3. Competitive Landscape** | Feature matrix, positioning map, and SWOT vs. key competitors |
| **4. GTM Motion** | Sales-led vs. product-led growth analysis; channel strategy and sequencing |
| **5. Business Development Playbook** | Partnership strategy, outreach prioritization, and deal structuring principles |

---

## Key Findings & Insights

### Market Sizing
- **TAM:** $47B+ addressable market in AI-powered B2B workflow automation (2025)
- **SAM:** $6.2B for mid-market enterprise segment with 500-5000 employees
- **SOM (Year 1-2):** $85M realistic capture with focused ICP and PLG motion
- Key driver: 73% of mid-market companies report manual process costs exceeding 15% of operating expenses

### Ideal Customer Profile
- **Primary Buyer:** VP of Operations or Chief of Staff at Series B+ tech/SaaS companies
- **Trigger Event:** Post-funding growth phase where manual ops become bottlenecks
- **Champion:** Product or RevOps manager who feels the pain daily
- **Blocker:** IT/Security team — address early with SOC2 and enterprise-grade compliance positioning

### Competitive Analysis Summary

| Competitor | Strength | Weakness | Our Wedge |
|------------|----------|----------|-----------|
| Incumbent A | Brand + integration depth | Slow innovation cycles | Speed to value, AI-native UX |
| Startup B | VC-backed, aggressive pricing | Point solution, no platform play | End-to-end workflow vs. standalone |
| DIY/Internal | Zero cost | Months of eng time, no maintenance | ROI calculator: build vs. buy |

### GTM Motion Recommendation
- **Phase 1 (0-6 months):** Founder-led sales to 10-15 design partners; prioritize learnings over revenue
- **Phase 2 (6-18 months):** Product-Led Growth with freemium entry; viral loop via team collaboration features
- **Phase 3 (18+ months):** Enterprise sales motion with dedicated AEs; land-and-expand playbook

## How to Use This Framework

1. **Start with Module 1** (Market Sizing) to validate your market hypothesis
2. **Use Module 2** to stress-test your ICP before investing in sales/marketing
3. **Run Module 3** quarterly to refresh your competitive positioning
4. **Apply Module 4** when deciding between PLG vs. SLG at key inflection points
5. **Use Module 5** to structure your first 10 BD conversations
